Trick or Treat Studios and The Steve Miller Band Bring Back a 41 Year Old Rock N Roll Icon - The Joker Mask!

Released in 1973, The Joker was the eighth album by Steve Miller Band. The artwork features Steve Miller wearing a green robot mask, which has become one of two mascots for the band and is considered among the greatest album covers ever produced.  In fact, Rolling Stone Magazine ranked The Joker as one of the "Top 100 Album Covers Of All Time".

In honor of the 41-year anniversary of the album, Steve Miller contacted Trick or Treat Studios to reproduce the famous Joker Mask icon.

Sculpted by Erich Lubatti, The Joker now lives again and is currently being sold at venues across the world as part of Steve’s summer tour with Journey.

World Famous Sculptor & Motion Picture Director William Malone Joins Trick or Treat Studios!

Trick or Treat Studios is honored to have one of our all time favorite Halloween Mask Sculptors and Motion Picture Director, William Malone, on our Sculpting Team!

Since the age of 14, Malone has been making movies and designing monster masks for Halloween.

A legend in the mask making world, Malone was responsible for designing and sculpting the mask used by the Horror Icon, Michael Myers, in John Carpenter's Classic, Halloween (1978).

After attending classes at UCLA to study directing Malone wrote and directed the sci-fi horror shocker Scared to Death (1980). In 1984, Malone directed Creature (1985), which was nominated for a Saturn Award at the 1985 Academy of Science Fiction and Horror films.

Malone spent the next 14 years directing episodes for the TV series Freddy's Nightmares, the HBO series Tales From the Crypt, Sleepwalkers and a made for TV movie W.I.E.R.D. World. In 1999, Joel Silver and Robert Zemeckis hired Malone to direct the remake of House on Haunted Hill and in 2002, Malone directed his own feature Feardotcom. In 2005 Malone was invited by Showtime Masters of Horror creator Mick Garris to direct an episode for the series titled The Fair Haired Child.

Needless to say, Trick or Treat Studios is honored to have Malone on our sculpting team!

Neal Kennemore Joins Trick or Treat Studios and Debuts With Ol' Scratch!

We are absolutely giddy to have the amazing Neal Kennemore be part of our sculpting team!

Neal is a third generation sculptor working in the Film, TV, Halloween and Collectable industry. Born in Burbank, CA at Saint Joseph Hospital, right across the street from Disney Animation Studios.

Neal’s bloodline in sculpting started with his Grandfather, the legendary Disney and Creature FX Sculptor and Designer Michael McCracken. Followed by his Father, the well known Creature FX Sculptor Jeff Kennemore, whose sculpting work was featured in John Carpenter's The Thing, Twilight Zone: The Movie, and Poltergeist II, just to name a few.
